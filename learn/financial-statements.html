The Purpose of Financial Statements

Financial statements are structured reports that summarize a company's financial activities and position. They serve as the final output of the accounting process and provide essential information to various stakeholders.

The Three Main Financial Statements

Statement Purpose Time Frame
Income Statement (Profit & Loss) Shows revenues and expenses to determine profitability For a period (e.g., month, quarter, year)
Statement of Financial Position (Balance Sheet) Shows assets, liabilities, and equity As of a specific date
Cash Flow Statement Shows cash inflows and outflows For a period (e.g., month, quarter, year)

1. Income Statement (Profit & Loss)

The Income Statement shows whether a business is profitable by reporting revenues earned and expenses incurred during a specific period.

How Double Entry Connects to Financial Statements

Double entry bookkeeping ensures that financial statements are accurate and consistent:

  • Balance Sheet Equation: The double entry system guarantees that Assets = Liabilities + Equity
  • Income Statement: Revenue and expense accounts correctly flow to the Income Statement
  • Retained Profits: Net income from the Income Statement increases Retained Profits on the Balance Sheet
  • Cash Flow Statement: Changes in balance sheet accounts help determine cash flows
